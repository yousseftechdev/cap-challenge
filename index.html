Capstone Big Idea

Bridges allow vehicles and pedestrians to cross water streams safely and quickly without ferries, reducing risks and promoting connectivity. They support emergency response, trade, tourism, and reduce transportation costs.

Essential Question

How can we design and construct bridges to cross water streams while allowing ships to cross efficiently?

Design Challenge

Your team will design and construct a bridge with fixed and movable parts using a truss structure for the fixed part. Explore truss bridge designs and build and test a prototype with different bridge components, including a movable section for ship passage.

Design Requirements

  • Scale: 1:50, Length: 40-50 meters
  • Two traffic lanes (3.5 m each) and two pedestrian lanes (1.5 m each) with a 1 m island in the middle
  • Clearance of 5 m between water and bridge deck
  • Movable section for ships 12-14 m wide, 10 m high, operated mechanically or hydraulically
  • Bridge must support 10 kg load with max deflection of 5 cm
  • Prototype weight limit: 20 Kg

Constraints

  • Use palm tree parts for bridge components, diameter up to 5 mm, length up to 150 mm
  • Use adhesives for assembly, follow safety rules, and perform lab tests as needed
  • Fixed span minimum: 12m (no internal columns)

Proposal

We settled for a vertical-lift bridge design, it works by having a movable section in the middle that is lifted upwards using ropes and motors.

  1. Materials needed:
    • Palm tree midrib (Recycled): Makes up the entire structure of the bridge.

    • Adhesives (Glue): Sticks everything together.

    • Lubricant (Oil or grease): Sticks Eases the passage of the moving part through the paths in the adjacent poles.

  2. Electronics needed:
    • ESP32 (405 EGP): Used to controll the opening and closing of the bridge.

    • Motor driver (80 EGP): Takes signals from the ESP to turn the motor on or off and control the direction of rotation.

    • 3-12V DC Motor (12-20 EGP): Pulls the rope to move and open the middle sectio of the bridge.

    • External Power supply (Reused): Powers the motor instead of the ESP since it can't give the power needed to turn on the motor.

    • Jumper wires (1.25 EGP/wire): Connects all the components together.
